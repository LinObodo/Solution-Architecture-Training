# Week 1 Day 2 – What Is Solution Architecture

## Definition of Solution Architecture
Solution Architecture is the practice of designing a technology solution that addresses a specific business problem. It defines how systems, applications, data, and processes work together to deliver business value.

---

## Purpose of Solution Architecture in IT Projects
Solution Architecture provides structure and direction before implementation begins. Its purpose is to ensure that IT solutions are aligned with business goals, technically feasible, and designed to reduce delivery risk, rework, and failure.

---

## Business Problems vs Technical Solutions
Business problems describe what the organization needs to achieve, such as improving efficiency or reducing delays.  
Technical solutions describe how technology is used to achieve those outcomes.

Solution Architecture ensures that technical solutions directly address real business problems, rather than implementing technology for its own sake.

---

## High‑Level System Thinking
High‑level system thinking focuses on viewing the solution as a whole instead of isolated components. It considers system interactions, data flow, integrations, and dependencies to ensure consistency and avoid fragmented solutions.

---

## Real‑World Examples
Examples of Solution Architecture include:
- Designing a customer portal that integrates frontend applications, backend services, and databases
- Automating a business workflow that spans multiple systems
- Introducing a new system that must integrate with existing legacy platforms

---

## Summary
This assignment introduces Solution Architecture as a discipline focused on solving business problems through high‑level system design. It establishes the foundation for understanding how business needs are translated into technical solutions.
